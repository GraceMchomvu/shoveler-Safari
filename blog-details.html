Best Time to Visit the Serengeti: A Guide from Arusha

From our base in Arusha, SHOVELER SAFARI designs personalized Northern Circuit journeys for travellers who want more than a checklist. The Serengeti is spectacular year-round — but the experience changes with the seasons. Here is how we help guests choose the right window for wildebeest migration, big cats, birding, or a quieter bush escape.

June to October brings the dry season: shorter grass, clearer sightings, and the famous river crossings in the western and northern Serengeti. December to March is calving season on the southern plains — predators gather, and photography is exceptional. The green season (April–May) means fewer vehicles, lush landscapes, and excellent value for couples and families who prefer a slower pace.

There is no single “perfect” month — only the right safari for you. Tell us what you dream of seeing, and we build the route around it.

SHOVELER SAFARI · Arusha, Tanzania

Most of our guests combine Serengeti with Ngorongoro Crater, Tarangire’s elephant country, and Lake Manyara — then finish with Zanzibar’s beaches. We handle lodge selection, park fees, private guides, and flexible day-to-day pacing so you can focus on the wildlife. Explore packages on www.shovelersafari.com or message us on WhatsApp for a free quote.

What to Pack & What to Expect

Pack soft earth tones, a warm layer for early game drives, binoculars, and a camera with a zoom lens if you love photography. Mornings start cool on the crater rim and open plains; afternoons warm quickly. Our guides brief you each evening on the next day’s plan — migration movement, predator activity, and the best light for photos.
